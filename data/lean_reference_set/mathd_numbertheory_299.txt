-- Proof content:
-- 1. [Problem Restatement] Find the units digit of the product 1 · 3 · 5 · 7 · 9 · 11 · 13.
-- 2. [Key Idea] Once the factor 5 appears, every remaining factor is odd, and 5 × (odd) always ends in 5. So the units digit stays 5.
-- 3. [Proof] Work modulo 10. First, 1 · 3 ≡ 3 (mod 10). Then 3 · 5 ≡ 15 ≡ 5 (mod 10). From this point on, multiplying by any odd number keeps the last digit 5: 5 · 7 ≡ 35 ≡ 5, 5 · 9 ≡ 45 ≡ 5, 5 · 11 ≡ 55 ≡ 5, 5 · 13 ≡ 65 ≡ 5 (all modulo 10). Therefore, the final product is congruent to 5 modulo 10.
-- 4. [Conclusion] The units digit of 1 · 3 · 5 · 7 · 9 · 11 · 13 is 5.
