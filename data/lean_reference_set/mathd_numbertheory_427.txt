-- Proof content:
-- 1. [Problem Restatement] Find the sum of the distinct prime divisors of the sum of all positive divisors of 500.
-- 2. [Key Idea] Compute the divisor-sum σ(500) using the multiplicative formula σ(n) = ∏ (p_i^(a_i+1) − 1)/(p_i − 1) for n = ∏ p_i^{a_i}. Then factor σ(500) and add its distinct prime factors.
-- 3. [Proof] 
   a) Factor 500: 500 = 2^2 · 5^3.
   b) Apply the divisor-sum formula: σ(500) = σ(2^2) · σ(5^3) = (2^3 − 1)/(2 − 1) · (5^4 − 1)/(5 − 1) = (8 − 1)/1 · (625 − 1)/4 = 7 · 156 = 1092.
   c) Factor 1092: 1092 = 2 · 546 = 2^2 · 273 = 2^2 · 3 · 91 = 2^2 · 3 · 7 · 13.
   d) The distinct prime divisors of 1092 are 2, 3, 7, and 13. Their sum is 2 + 3 + 7 + 13 = 25.
-- 4. [Conclusion] The sum of the distinct prime divisors of σ(500) is 25.
