-- Proof content:
-- 1. [Problem Restatement] We define three quantities: separate_surface_area = 6 × (1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + 4^2 + 5^2 + 6^2 + 7^2), hidden_area = 2 × (1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + 4^2 + 5^2 + 6^2), and exposed_surface_area = separate_surface_area − hidden_area. The goal is to show that exposed_surface_area = 658.

-- 2. [Key Idea] Compute the sums of squares explicitly, then carry out the multiplications and subtraction to obtain the final value.

-- 3. [Proof] 
- First compute the sums of squares:
  1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + 4^2 + 5^2 + 6^2 + 7^2 = 140,
  1^2 + 2^2 + 3^2 + 4^2 + 5^2 + 6^2 = 91.
- Multiply by the given coefficients:
  separate_surface_area = 6 × 140 = 840,
  hidden_area = 2 × 91 = 182.
- Subtract to get the exposed area:
  exposed_surface_area = 840 − 182 = 658.
- Therefore, substituting these computed values confirms the desired equality.

-- 4. [Conclusion] The exposed surface area equals 658.
