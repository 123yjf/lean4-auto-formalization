-- 1. [Problem Restatement] For all real numbers x, prove that (3x − 2)(4x + 1) − (3x − 2)(4x) + 1 = 3x − 1.
-- 2. [Key Idea] Factor the common term (3x − 2) from the difference of products using distributivity: a·b − a·c = a·(b − c). Then note that (4x + 1) − 4x = 1, and simplify.
-- 3. [Proof] Consider (3x − 2)(4x + 1) − (3x − 2)(4x). By distributivity, this equals (3x − 2)((4x + 1) − 4x). The inner difference simplifies to 1, so the whole expression becomes (3x − 2)·1 + 1 = 3x − 2 + 1 = 3x − 1.
-- 4. [Conclusion] The identity holds: (3x − 2)(4x + 1) − (3x − 2)(4x) + 1 = 3x − 1 for all real x.
