-- Proof content:
-- 1. [Problem Restatement] Show that the product
--    (sum over k = 0..19 of log base 5^(k+1) of 3^((k+1)^2))
--    times
--    (sum over k = 0..99 of log base 9^(k+1) of 25^(k+1))
--    equals 21000.

-- 2. [Key Idea] Use the definition logb a b = (log b)/(log a) and the logarithm power rule log(x^n) = n·log x (for x > 0), so that
--    - logb(5^(k+1))(3^((k+1)^2)) simplifies to (k+1)·logb(5)(3),
--    - logb(9^(k+1))(25^(k+1)) simplifies to logb(3)(5), independent of k,
--    and use the reciprocal identity logb a b · logb b a = 1. Then evaluate the arithmetic sums.

-- 3. [Proof]
--    - First sum. For each k,
--      logb(5^(k+1))(3^((k+1)^2)) = [log(3^((k+1)^2))]/[log(5^(k+1))]
--      = [((k+1)^2)·log 3]/[((k+1))·log 5]
--      = (k+1) · (log 3 / log 5)
--      = (k+1) · logb 5 3.
--      Therefore
--      ∑_{k=0}^{19} logb(5^(k+1))(3^((k+1)^2))
--      = (logb 5 3) · ∑_{k=0}^{19} (k+1)
--      = (logb 5 3) · (1 + 2 + … + 20)
--      = (logb 5 3) · 210.
--      Hence the first sum equals 210 · logb 5 3.

--    - Second sum. For each k,
--      logb(9^(k+1))(25^(k+1)) = [log(25^(k+1))]/[log(9^(k+1))]
--      = [(k+1)·log 25]/[(k+1)·log 9] = log 25 / log 9.
--      Using 25 = 5^2 and 9 = 3^2, we get
--      log 25 / log 9 = (2·log 5)/(2·log 3) = log 5 / log 3 = logb 3 5.
--      Hence every term equals logb 3 5, so
--      ∑_{k=0}^{99} logb(9^(k+1))(25^(k+1)) = 100 · logb 3 5.

--    - Product of change-of-base factors. By definition,
--      logb 5 3 · logb 3 5 = (log 3 / log 5) · (log 5 / log 3) = 1.

--    - Putting it all together:
--      (210 · logb 5 3) · (100 · logb 3 5) = 210 · 100 · 1 = 21000.

--    All logarithm identities used are valid since the bases and arguments (3, 5, 9, 25, and their positive powers) are positive and bases are not 1, and k+1 > 0 for the indices considered.

-- 4. [Conclusion] The given product equals 21000.
